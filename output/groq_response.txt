**Benefits and Potential Health Risks of Ingredients:**

**Refined Wheat Flour (Maida) and Instant Noodles Powder:**

Benefits:
- Provides carbohydrates and energy
- May be used as a thickening agent in recipes

Potential Health Risks:
- Refined carbohydrates can cause a spike in blood sugar and insulin levels
- High glycemic index may contribute to weight gain, insulin resistance, and type 2 diabetes
- May contain additives and preservatives that can be detrimental to overall health

**Iodised Salt:**

Benefits:
- Essential for thyroid function and iodine deficiency prevention
- Helps in preventing goitre, a goiter or swelling of the thyroid gland

Potential Health Risks:
- Consuming excessive amounts may lead to increased risk of thyroid disorders
- Contains additives like potassium iodate, which may cause gastrointestinal issues

**Acidity Regulators (INS 500):**

Benefits:
- Used to maintain food pH levels
- Helps extend shelf life and preserve food quality

Potential Health Risks:
- Can cause digestive issues, such as bloating and gas, in some individuals
- Some studies suggest a link between certain acidity regulators and increased risk of cancer; however, more research is needed to confirm this.

**Stabilizer (INS 451):**

Benefits:
- May improve food texture and stability
- Used to prolong shelf life

Potential Health Risks:
- Some studies suggest a link between certain stabilizers and increased risk of certain cancers, but more research is needed to confirm this.

**Flavours and Colourants:**

Benefits:
- Adds taste and aroma to food
- Enhances food appearance

Potential Health Risks:
- Certain flavors, such as natural flavoring substances, may not be regulated as strictly as other additives
- Some artificial colors may be associated with hyperactivity in children
- May cause allergic reactions or intolerance in some individuals

**Spices (Tasty Masala):**

Benefits:
- May have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties
- Gives flavor and aroma to the dish

Potential Health Risks:
- Excessive consumption may lead to an imbalance in essential nutrients
- Some spices, such as coriander, may cause digestive issues in individuals with sensitivities
- Fenugreek may interact with certain medications, such as blood Thinners

**Palmolein Oil:**

Benefits:
- Rich in monounsaturated fats, which may help lower cholesterol levels
- May have antioxidant properties

Potential Health Risks:
- High in saturated fats, which may increase the risk of heart disease
- Can cause digestive issues, such as diarrhea, in some individuals

**Sugar:**

Benefits:
- Provides energy
- May be used as a sweetener in recipes

Potential Health Risks:
- Consuming excessive amounts may lead to an increased risk of dental cavities, obesity, and type 2 diabetes
- May be detrimental to overall health when part of a diet high in processed foods

**Edible Corn Starch:**

Benefits:
- Used as a thickening agent in recipes
- Gluten-free

Potential Health Risks:
- May cause gastrointestinal issues, such as bloating and gas, in some individuals

**Yeast Extract Powder:**

Benefits:
- Rich in cysteine, an amino acid that may help lower blood pressure
- May improve digestion

Potential Health Risks:
- Excessive consumption may lead to an imbalance in essential nutrients
- May cause digestive issues, such as bloating and gas, in some individuals

**Hydrolysed Vegetable Protein (Soya):**

Benefits:
- May be a suitable alternative to animal-derived protein sources for vegetarians and vegans
- May help lower cholesterol levels

 Potential Health Risks:
- May be contaminated with phytoestrogens from soybeans
- Some research suggests a link between-consuming large amounts of soya protein and an increased risk of certain cancers; however, more research is needed to confirm this.

**Citric Acid (INS 330):**

Benefits:
- Preserves food quality and shelf life
- Helps lower pH levels in food

Potential Health Risks:
- Can cause digestive issues, such as bloating and gas, in some individuals
- May interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners

**Malic Acid (INS 296):**

Benefits:
- Used to lower pH levels in food
- Preserves food quality and shelf life

Potential Health Risks:
- May cause digestive issues, such as bloating and gas, in some individuals
- Can interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners

**Other Considerations:**

- The USP (United States Pharmacopeia) symbol and the net weight included of all ingredients generally provide some information about the product's quality and quantity of active ingredients.
- The "Use by" date is a guide to ensure the product is consumed within a certain timeframe, reducing the risk of foodborne illness.

**Recommendations:**

- Consume noodles in moderation, as part of a balanced diet
- Be mindful of portion sizes and the ingredients used
- Avoid excessive consumption of refined carbohydrates and added sugars
- Consider alternative ingredients, such as whole wheat flour and natural sweeteners
- Seek advice from a healthcare professional or registered dietitian for personalized recommendations.
